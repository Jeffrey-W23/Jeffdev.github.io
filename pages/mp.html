INTRODUCTION


Mirror Planet (Along with Millionaire Tycoon: World) is one of the first professional projects I've worked on as a games developer as a part of my work at SavySoda. I've worked on this project from the pre-development stages, working with a small team of great people and getting a real taste of how game development works inside the Australian games industry.


I've spent three years living and breathing Mirror Planet, learning more about Unity than ever before, especially in the Unity UI System! Along with a greater understanding of Unity, I've also experienced working on a multi-platformed mobile release for iOS and Android, working from the development side of cryptocurrency with my work on an on-chain wallet, and I've got a taste for games design, helping come up with features and new directions.



ROLES AND RESPONSIBILITIES


My role at SavySoda is as one of the main client developers working on Mirror Planet and Millionaire Tycoon: World, spending my day-to-day fixing bugs, updating UI and adding new features.

As this project is still in early development, I can't talk about the specific details of the development process, but I will try and give an idea of what I've worked on in each area of the project.


Programming:
  • I've worked as one of the main programmers on Mirror Planet, developing new features, resolving bugs, and up-keeping code.

UI & UX:
  • One of my central development roles with Mirror Planet is UI & UX throughout the entire project, working with artists to get their visions from mockup design to functioning reality. I worked on the front end of the UI in Unity, along with the backend in code.

Game Design:
  • I've worked with other developers on our team as a part of the design process for Mirror Planet, attending weekly meetings to discuss the directions and how we would design new features.

Community Moderation:
  • As a part of one of our first pre-alpha launches, I worked as the lead social and community moderator for our Discord server. This Discord server's main purpose was for our customer support and bug/feature reporting, where I spoke with users one-on-one to resolve issues and take suggestions.
