ABOUT ME


I'm a hard-working, passionate games programmer that is currently living in Melbourne Australia. Over the last 3 years, I have been studying at the Academy of Interactive Entertainment (AIE) to obtain a Bachelor of Games and Virtual Worlds (Programming).

My passion for programming started with a small community of developers and artist, attempting to recreate the video game Journey to the centre of Hawkthorne, shown in episode 3.20 of NBC's Community. I contributed to the project back in 2012 with the creation of various sprite-sheets (Abed: Cowboy, Jeff: Heather Popandlocklear, Jeff: Mercury Poisoning) for different characters and skins. Sparking my curiosity, and soon pushing me towards exploring and experimenting with various scripts throughout the project.

My experience with the Hawkthorne community is an important step in my development journey, as it leads directly to my discovery of the Love2D engine, which I used to develop my first programming project. This project was Zombie Game, a project that ignited a fire in me that didn't stop for 3 straight months of development, only slowing down for a job at my local Mc Donalds.

After the development of Zombie Game, I applied for a traineeship position at a local software development company known as Advance Computing. I gained many important skills at Advance, made many great friends and got my first real start in programming. This traineeship pushed me towards my university degree at the Academy Of Interactive Entertainment (I have learnt more than I could ever imagine here, I can't praise this academy enough for the inspiration it has given me for games and programming), the rest is history!
